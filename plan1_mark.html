Entry Signal Criteria
1. Higher High
Most recent resistance level (swing high) is higher than the previous resistance level, looking back ~2 months to identify significant peaks
2. Pullback (2–5 days)
Pullback starts when a day's high is lower than the prior day's high. The day with the lowest low during the pullback is the key reference day. The pullback continues while each subsequent day's high stays below that day's high + $0.20. If a new day has a lower low, it becomes the new reference day and the threshold resets. Downward trend is over when any day's high reaches the reference day's high + $0.20 or more.
3. Near Support
EMA20, SMA50, SMA200, Fib 38.2/50/61.8%, swing low
4. Higher Low
Most recent support level (swing low) is higher than the previous support level, looking back ~2 months to identify significant troughs
